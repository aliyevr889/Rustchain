# Grandma's Guide with Rustchain

Hello! If you're reading this, you might be wondering what "Rustchain" is and why your old computer is suddenly important again. Don't worry, we'll explain it simply!

## What is Rustchain?
Rustchain is a digital network (like a registry or a bank) that runs on many computers at once. Most modern networks want the fastest, newest computers. **Rustchain is different.** It actually likes older computers!

## Proof of Antiquity
We have a special rule called **Proof of Antiquity**. It means that if your computer is older but still works well, it's actually *more* valuable to the network. Think of it like a classic car—the older and better maintained it is, the more respect it gets.

## Why use my old computer?
By letting your old computer help the Rustchain network, you are helping to keep the digital world decentralized (spreading the power around instead of letting one big company own everything).

## How do I get started?
1. **Plug it in**: Make sure your old computer is safely connected to the power.
2. **Connect to the Internet**: It needs to be able to talk to other computers.
3. **Run the software**: A small program will run in the background. It won't make your computer slower than it already is!

## Multipliers & Rewards
Because we love "vintage" tech, we give extra points (multipliers) for certain types of old hardware:
*   **PowerPC G4 (2001-2005):** 2.5x
*   **Power Mac G5 (2003-2006):** 2.0x
*   **Pentium 4 / NetBurst (2000-2006):** 1.5x
*   **Core 2 / Athlon 64 (2005-2008):** 1.3x
*   **Nehalem (2008-2010):** 1.2x
*   **Sandy Bridge (2011-2012):** 1.1x
*   **Haswell (2013-2015):** 1.1x
*   **Broadwell / Skylake (2015-2017):** 1.05x
*   **Modern PCs (2020+):** 1.0x (Baseline)

## Time Decay
*Note: These bonuses gradually decrease by 15% each year as the network grows, to reward the people who joined us early!*

Thank you for helping keep the history of computing alive while building the future!
